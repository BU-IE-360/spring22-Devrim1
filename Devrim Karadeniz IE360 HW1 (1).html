Introduction¶

In this assignment, I tried to visualize the data to examine which data are related to the number of monthly tourists coming to Turkey apart from the seasons. I got the data that may be related to the monthly number of tourists coming to Turkey from EVDS on the website of the Central Bank of the Republic of Turkey and Google Trends, a service provided by Google. I paid attention to the fact that all the data is between 2018 and 2022 and is divided on a monthly basis. I converted data that is not in this format to this format. I thought of the Dollar/TL parity, the number of times Turkey Hotel was searched on Google and the Brent Petrol price in Europe as data that could be related to the amount of tourists coming to Turkey monthly. Between the years 2018-2022, I obtained the data about the monthly amount of tourists coming to Turkey, the monthly status of the dollar/TL parity and the monthly dollar price of Brent Petrol through the EVDS system. I also obtained the data on the trend of the phrase "Turkey Hotels" through Google Trends.

  xlab("Years") + ylab("Number of tourists coming to Turkey")+ ggtitle("Number of tourists coming to Turkey Between 2018 - 2022")+
  theme(legend.position = "none")

EVDS.data %>%
  ggplot(aes(x= Date, y = Number.of.Tourists)) +
  geom_line(color = 1) + 
  labs(title = " Number of tourists coming to Turkey Between 2018 - 2022", x = "Time (Monthly)", y = "Number of tourists coming to Turkey") +
  scale_x_date(date_breaks = "6 month", date_labels = "%Y %b", date_minor_breaks = "1 month") + 
  theme(axis.text.x=element_text(angle=60, hjust=1.4, vjust = 1.4))

I used 3 different visualization techniques to evaluate the data from different aspects. As can be seen, there is a seasonality effect in the amount of tourists coming to Turkey. More Tourists visits in the summers and springs, and less in the winters and autumns. Let's remember that the reason for the sudden decrease in March 2020 is the Pandemic. Let's not forget that this data is also under the influence of the pandemic. Besides, we can say that before the pandemic, apart from the seasonality effect, there was an increasing trend in the number of tourists coming to Turkey.

Conclusion¶

It is possible to establish a connection between the amount of Brent Petrol Price and the phrase "Turkey Hotels" searched on Google and the number of tourists visiting Turkey. This interpretation is easily made by looking at the graphics, but it is not possible to explain the relationship between the Dollar/TL value and the number of Tourists just by looking at the graphics. Besides, it is possible to say that the pandemic period has affected many things. For a more accurate assessment, the data should be freed from the effect of the pandemic. Apart from that, the Seasonality effect makes it difficult to establish a relationship between the data. In order to establish more accurate relationships, the data should also be purified from the seasonility effect.
